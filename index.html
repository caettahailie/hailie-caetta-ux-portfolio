About me

I’m a UX researcher and designer studying Human Systems Engineering with a UX concentration. I’m really interested in how people make decisions—especially when systems are confusing, stressful, or trying to be “helpful” but miss the mark.

I like digging into the why behind user behavior. Why do people trust certain systems? When do they stop questioning information? Where does frustration actually start? Those questions usually guide my work more than jumping straight to solutions.

I’m motivated by work that makes technology feel less intimidating and more supportive—especially in moments when users need clarity, reassurance, or control.

My projects span travel platforms, media apps, AI tools, and service experiences. Whether I’m running usability tests, mapping task flows, or designing interfaces, my goal is always the same: make things clearer, more honest, and easier to navigate in real moments.

Outside of UX, I’m usually cooking, watching long-form YouTube videos, or planning my next trip. I’m drawn to things that tell a story or help me understand how people think, whether that’s through food, media, or travel.
